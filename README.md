# Privacy Policy for Pouch Cell Inspector

**Last Updated:** March 10, 2026

## Introduction

Pouch Cell Inspector is a mobile application designed to support the visual inspection of lithium-ion pouch cells. The app allows users to inspect pouch cells using their device camera, review inspection results, and use on-device analysis tools to assist with evaluation.

This application is also designed with accessibility in mind, including support for users who are visually impaired or have low vision through features such as speech output, adjustable appearance settings, and haptic feedback.

This Privacy Policy explains how information is handled when you use our application.

This app was conducted as part of a research project at the University of Michigan Dearborn, designed to support engineers, educators, visually impaired users, and enthusiast who are interested in learning about pouch cells

## Overview of the Application

Pouch Cell Inspector is a tool for visual inspection and review of lithium-ion pouch cells. Depending on how the app is used, users may:

- Use the device camera to inspect or review pouch cells
- Capture images for inspection and analysis
- Review inspection and analysis results generated on the device
- Adjust preferences such as speech output, appearance, haptics, and photo-saving behavior


The core functionality of the app is designed to operate locally on your device.

## Accessibility

Pouch Cell Inspector is designed to improve usability for a broad range of users, including users who are blind, visually impaired, or have low vision. The app may include accessibility-related features such as:

- Speech output or spoken feedback
- Adjustable appearance settings to improve readability and visibility
- Haptic feedback to support non-visual interaction
- Compatibility with device-level accessibility features provided by iOS

These features are intended to help users interact with the application more effectively while keeping app activity and processing on the device.

## Information We Collect

Pouch Cell Inspector is designed with privacy in mind. The application does not collect, transmit, sell, or share personal information or inspection data with external servers as part of its core functionality.

In general, the app does **not** collect or send to us:

- Photos or inspection images
- Inspection results
- Machine learning analysis results
- Usage analytics
- Advertising identifiers
- Tracking data
- Contact lists
- Location data
- Audio recordings
- Cloud backups created by the app itself

Information generated while using the app remains on the user's device unless the user explicitly chooses to save, export, or share it.

## Images, Photo Library Access, and Saving Behavior

Pouch Cell Inspector may allow users to capture or review images as part of the inspection process. The app is designed so that images used for inspection are processed locally on the device.

By default, the app does **not** save pictures to the user's photo library unless the user has enabled that option in the app's settings or otherwise chooses to save an image.

More specifically:

- The app does not automatically save captured inspection images to the device photo library unless the user enables photo-saving behavior
- The app does not upload photos to external servers
- The app does not transmit photos for remote processing
- If users choose to save images to their photo library, those images are stored under the user's control on their own device
- If users choose to export or share images, that action is initiated by the user, not automatically by the app

## Data Generated Within the App

While using the application, users may generate certain types of information, including:

- Inspection results
- Images captured for analysis
- Notes or observations entered by the user
- Analysis results produced by the on-device machine learning model
- App preference settings such as speech output, appearance, haptics, and photo-saving behavior

This information is stored locally on the user's device and is not transmitted to external servers by the application as part of normal operation.

## On-Device Machine Learning and Local Processing

Pouch Cell Inspector uses on-device machine learning and local processing to assist with the inspection and analysis of lithium-ion pouch cells.

Important privacy considerations:

- The machine learning model runs entirely on the user's device
- Images and inspection data are processed locally on-device
- No inspection images or analysis results are sent to external servers by the app
- The app does not rely on cloud processing for its core inspection functionality

Because processing occurs locally on the device, inspection data remains under the user's control and does not leave the device unless the user explicitly chooses to save, export, or share it.

## Data Storage

All data generated within the application is stored locally on the user's device.

The application does not maintain a remote database or cloud storage system for core inspection data. Users maintain control over locally stored information and may delete it through the app, through device settings, or by uninstalling the application.

## Data Sharing

Pouch Cell Inspector does not sell, rent, or share user data with third parties.

The application does not include:

- Third-party analytics services
- Advertising networks
- External tracking tools for core app use

Any data created in the application remains under the control of the user unless the user chooses to export or share it.

## Third-Party Services

The application's core functionality does not depend on third-party services that collect user inspection data.

Inspection, image handling, and machine learning analysis are intended to occur locally on the device.

## Children's Privacy

Pouch Cell Inspector is not directed toward children under the age of 13. The application does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

If you believe that personal information from a child may have been provided through the app, please contact us so that appropriate action can be taken.

## Security

We take privacy and security seriously. Because the application is designed not to transmit or externally store inspection data as part of its core functionality, the risk of unauthorized remote access is reduced.

Users are responsible for maintaining the security of their own devices and controlling access to the information stored locally on those devices.

## Changes to This Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. When updates are made, the “Last Updated” date at the top of this document will be revised.

Users are encouraged to review this Privacy Policy periodically.
